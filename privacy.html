Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-09-13 04:00 UTC

This Privacy Policy explains how the Workday Timer widget (“Service”) handles data when you connect your Notion workspace.

What we access

  • Notion databases named Clients, Projects, and Time Log (or IDs you configure).
  • Basic metadata necessary to create/read time entries you request.

What we store

  • Access token: stored locally in localStorage in your browser. It is sent with each request to run the action you asked for (e.g., “log time”).
  • We do not persist your token or content on our server. Netlify may process requests transiently to deliver the function.

What we write to Notion

When you click Pause/Stop, we create a row in your Time Log with date, minutes, and relations to the chosen Project and Client. We may also create those items only if your configuration allows it (your current build uses “projects-only”, which does not create items).

Analytics & cookies

No analytics are built in. The widget uses localStorage for preferences (theme, weekly goal, most recent selection). No third‑party cookies are set by the widget.

Subprocessors

  • Netlify – hosts this site and runs serverless functions.
  • Notion – API provider you authorize.

Data retention

We don’t keep copies of your workspace data. Your time entries live inside your Notion databases until you delete them.

Your rights

Because we don’t hold your personal data on our servers, requests to access or delete your data can be fulfilled by you directly in Notion (delete pages/rows) and by revoking access to the integration in Notion: Settings → My connections.
